Embroidery Services

Computerised needle and thread is one of the special techniques we recommend for branding of uniforms. The finish is of hig quality, hard wearing and often outlasts the life of the garment itself

Embroidery provides a distinctive look and can be done on a large rage of clothing and products including towels, bags, banners, hats and scarves.

Vinyl Heat Transfer Printing

Digitally printed and applied to the garment using high heat, vinyl printing produces vivid colours, crisp lines and a spectecular finish.

Vinyl transfers can be used to print designs onto surfaces where the usual methods may not be easy to implement, like hats. They produce high-quality results on almost any kind of fabric. Choose from a wide range of colours, and have numbers or letters cut out using our digital cutter. We can also provide special colour finishes i.e., gloss, metallic, reflective, fluoro and glitter.

Dye Sublimation Printing

Ideal for special finishes, printing photos or designs with a lot of detail and colours.

Dye sublimation printing (also known as all over printing) is a full colour, full-coverage printing technique. our artwork is printed onto paper and pressed onto the fabric using high heat. Colours are extremely vibrant and photo-realistic, with a durable and smooth feel. The design possibilities are therefore endless!
It is most effective on white or lightly coloured fabrics, and can print in full colour, including fluoro, metallic and reflective colours. It is commonly used in fashion clothing and sportswear.

Direct to Film Printing

Direct to film (DTF) heat transfers are a new and innovative way to brand your garments. Using a hybrid of water-based and digital inks.

DTF is durable with superb stretch and rebound, no cracking or fading, and is a much softer feel than digital printing or vinyl transfers. This makes it extremely versatile on multiple fabrics and seams. It also has the benefit of being eco-friendly – using water based inks and no PVC materials. It's considerably cheaper than direct to garment printing and faster to produce in large quantities. It's no wonder direct to film is considered the world's best heat transfer!
